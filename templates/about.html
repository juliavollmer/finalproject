Idea

As many people before me, I’m interested in the ways in which music can be translated into a visual output. My art teacher back in high school would always let us draw to music and while all our results were pretty abstract and wild, they shared similarities in the way they represented the rhythm and overall mood of the song. This inspired me for my project and research.

Research

In my project I want to connect music with images. Music in itself is not visual, but there are many tendencies to combine music with an image. Be it music videos, movies or paintings. As early as the 18th century people were already experimenting with color organs. Every key on the organ would correlate to a color. During the research the term ‘visual music’ came up. The term itself was first used in 1912 in order to describe paintings of Wassily Kandinsky, whose paintings were heavily influenced by music. Kandinksy tried to translate music into painting and create an experience similar to the experience of music. Abstract color patterns should evoke the impression of sound. Starting from the late 1920s people would experiment with moving images and make animations according to music. One prominent filmmaker in the field of abstract cinema was Oskar Fischinger (see https://youtu.be/they7m6YePo ). He would use abstract imagery to create music analogies. For example the appearance of a pattern would match the rhythm of the music. Another artist who focused more on ‘composing motion’ was Len Lye (see https://youtu.be/-DksmbDMDUU ). Nowadays with the help of computers the visual representation is often used in digital music players and live shows that feature light shows and other visual effects.
